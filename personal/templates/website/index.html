Neuronal development

How the brain is organized remains a central question in biology. Sensory axons are known to form ordered con­nec­tions in the brain in which the neighbor relationship between cells is preserved. This topographic mapping is thought to be essential for the efficient transfer of in­for­ma­tion. In my current research, I have identified a mol­e­cule required for maintaining topographic order in the mam­ma­li­an visual system.

Structure prediction

Interactions between complex carbohydrates other mac­ro­mol­e­cules are critical in several neurological processes and disease states. Unfortunately, het­er­o­ge­ne­i­ty and oth­er factors make these carbohydrates difficult to study, and our understanding of these interactions is often limited. To address this, I am developing methods to model these interactions computationally.
